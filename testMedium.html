How to land an internship at Goldman Sachs



How to land an internship at Goldman Sachs

Be unique. Be innovative. Be approachable. Be confident. Be smart.

About two months ago I wrote a blog post about my first hackathon, BigRed//Hacks, and how I was nervous and didn’t know what to expect. It turns out that BigRed//Hacks has been the most memorable (and successful) hackathon I ever attended.

During this hackathon I managed to get a Microsoft Azure BizSpark subscription for 3 years, and a subtle email (actually 2) that completely changed my life.

During the event, I made friends with a Microsoft evangelist from the Greater Rochester Area, and had an amazing conversation with a Goldman Sachs rep.

Honestly, at the time I wasn’t even thinking that our conversation could have led to anything. I was just excited to be able to talk to a couple of really interesting people representing cool companies.

Half a month after the hackathon (circa mid October) I got an email with the subject as “Campus Recruiting at Goldman Sachs”. It suggested that I apply to their engineering programs. My initial thought was that this was simply another generic email sent to everyone that went to BigRed//Hacks.

Hence I completely disregarded their invitation to apply online, I really just thought it was spam. One month later (2.5 months after the hackathon) I got another email, reminding me to apply if I had not done so already.

Something inside me told me I should do so. I figured if I had such an amazing experience talking to the Goldman Sachs rep at Cornell, I would have a good time working with them if I had the chance.

On a side note, one of my best friends works at a different investment bank and he was telling me their application process was extremely competitive. You can tell where I’m going with this, my hopes were not high at all.

Two weeks ago, as I was walking out of a startup in NYC, I got an email from a Goldman Sachs recruiter asking me if I was still interested in the position. At this point I was straight up confused. My application had been idle for four months and all of a sudden, at the end of the recruitment period for summer 2015, I get a follow up from the most prestigious bank in America.

I immediately said yes to my recruiter’s interest email, and my application process proceeded like everyone else’s. It turns out that my phone interview went well enough that I got elected to have an on-site superday interview a week ago.

Now I am an intern at Goldman Sachs and will be working with an incredible team of systems developers. What else could I’ve asked for?

Thank you BigRed//Hacks and everyone else that has mentored me so far throughout my computer science career.

I’ll Never stop advocating for youth, entrepreneurship, innovation, and development.


I consider myself lucky because I didn’t plan the unfolding of these events.

My advice for you is use your time wisely. Attend hackathons, innovate, code for fun, have fun besides coding, make friends.

Most importantly, be passionate of what you do, do it best, and be friendly and approachable at all times.

I’m so grateful to have this opportunity and I’m positive I’ll build things that matter.

What I learned throughout this experience is to follow your passions. Do not try to impress others. Instead, let everyone get impressed implicitly by your actions and behaviors.

I recommend having a very unique skill set if you’re trying to work for any department in a top tier company. But most importantly, be confident. Know who you are and know what you’re worth. Understand how valuable you can be to the world and never let anyone tell you otherwise.

If you manage to reflect these attributes throughout your interviews, I’m pretty confident you’ll land a very good job.

As of now, I’m in love with Goldman Sachs and I can wait for my term with them to begin.
